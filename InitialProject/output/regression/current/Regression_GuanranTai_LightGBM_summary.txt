LightGBM Regression (Ensemble) - Guanran Tai
=============================================

Objective: Electron energy regression (log-transformed, L1 loss)
Ensemble: 5 models with different random seeds
Training samples: 37718 electrons (filtered from 37800 total)

Data filtering:
  - Removed 82 electrons with E < 1000 MeV (0.22% of total electron sample)
  - These extreme low-energy outliers have inherently poor relative energy
    resolution and contribute ~15% of total MAE despite being <0.3% of data.
  - Rationale: ATLAS electron energy reconstruction resolution degrades
    significantly below ~2 GeV; these samples act as outliers that skew
    the relative MAE metric disproportionately. Filtering them improves
    model stability and overall generalization without affecting the
    physics validity of the prediction task.

Feature engineering:
  - Log-transform target: optimises for relative error naturally since
    MAE in log space approximates relative MAE in linear space
  - sqrt-inverse-energy sample weights: clip to [0.1, 5.0], gives
    higher weight to low-energy electrons to reduce their relative error
  - -999 sentinel values: kept as-is (LightGBM handles them natively)
  - Feature selection: mutual information + gain importance combined
    rank on dedicated holdout (20% of data), selects calorimeter energy
    variables over isolation variables for better energy estimation
